# Literary Society

The Literary Society serves as the central organization for students passionate about literature, creative writing, and literary analysis. Established 47 years ago, the society has a rich tradition of fostering literary appreciation and developing student writers across all disciplines.

## Regular Activities

Members of the Literary Society meet weekly for workshops, discussions, and presentations on various literary topics. Activities throughout the year include:

- Creative writing workshops led by faculty and visiting writers
- Critical analysis discussions of classic and contemporary works
- Student reading nights where members share their original works
- Field trips to literary events and conferences
- Publication opportunities in the society's journal, "Inkwell"

## Annual Gala

The highlight of the society's calendar is the Annual Literary Gala held each April. This elegant evening event brings together students, faculty, and alumni to celebrate outstanding achievements in creative writing and literary analysis. The gala features:

- Awards for excellence in various writing categories
- Selected student presentations of award-winning works
- Distinguished speakers from the literary world
- Distribution of the annual "Inkwell" publication

## Impact on Campus

The Literary Society plays a vital role in campus cultural life, promoting the value of thoughtful reading and writing across disciplines. Many alumni credit their involvement with helping to form professional connections that have furthered their careers in education, publishing, and the arts.

As the society's faculty advisor Dr. Elizabeth Harper notes, "The Literary Society provides a crucial space for students to develop both their creative voices and their critical faculties—skills that serve them well regardless of their chosen fields."
