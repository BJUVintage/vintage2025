# Hurricane Helene Response

When Hurricane Helene struck the region in September 2024, the campus community responded with remarkable resilience and compassion. The storm brought unprecedented rainfall and wind damage, temporarily disrupting campus operations and causing significant hardship throughout the surrounding counties. The University quickly activated its emergency response plan, converting the Field House into a temporary shelter for displaced students and community members. Campus Security, Facilities Management, and Student Life staff worked tirelessly to ensure safety and provide essential services during the three-day campus closure. Once immediate safety concerns were addressed, the focus shifted to recovery efforts. More than 800 students, faculty, and staff volunteered with clean-up and relief operations both on campus and in neighboring communities. The Student Government Association organized a successful fundraising campaign that provided emergency assistance to affected families. The University's quick response earned commendation from local officials, but more importantly, demonstrated the community's strength and commitment to service during crisis. The experience has led to enhanced disaster preparedness protocols and stronger partnerships with local emergency management agencies.
