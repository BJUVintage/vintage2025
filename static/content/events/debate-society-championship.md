# Debate Society Championship

The Debate Society's annual championship tournament took place on March 8, 2025, bringing together the university's most skilled debaters for a day of intellectual rigor and rhetorical excellence. The tournament, held in the Walker Lecture Hall, attracted a significant audience of students, faculty, and visiting debate enthusiasts.

## Tournament Format

### Preliminary Rounds
Eight finalist teams, having qualified through earlier competitions, participated in preliminary rounds debating topics ranging from environmental policy to international relations.

### Semi-Finals
The four highest-scoring teams advanced to the semi-finals, where they engaged with particularly challenging resolutions requiring both deep research and quick thinking.

### Championship Round
The final debate featured the teams of Jackson/Williams versus Thompson/Lee addressing the resolution: "Artificial intelligence regulation should prioritize innovation over restriction." The Thompson/Lee team emerged victorious through their exceptional argumentation and evidence presentation.

## Distinguished Judges
This year's championship panel featured:
- Professor Emeritus Dr. Samuel Wilson, former national debate champion
- The Honorable Judith Chambers, retired state supreme court justice
- Ms. Rebecca Liang, CEO of Wilson Communications and debate society alumna

## Individual Awards
- **Best Speaker**: Eliza Thompson
- **Most Improved Debater**: Marcus Davis
- **Outstanding Research**: Stephanie Lee
- **Spirit of Discourse Award**: Jackson Williams

## Reception
Following the awards ceremony, participants and attendees gathered for a reception where they continued discussions sparked by the tournament debates. Many alumni returned for this event, making it an excellent networking opportunity for current students.

## Impact on Campus
The championship tournament has become a significant campus tradition, highlighting the intellectual vitality of the university community. As debate society advisor Dr. Jamal Harris noted, "These students aren't just preparing for careers—they're learning to engage thoughtfully with the most challenging questions of our time."
