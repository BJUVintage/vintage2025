# Hurricane Helene


On the evening of September 26, 2024, Hurricane Helene made landfall on
the Florida coast. In a matter of hours, the storm reached the
Greenville area---and Bob Jones University---as a tropical storm.
Flooding and high winds caused extensive damage, including widespread
road closures and power outages that lasted more than a week in many
areas. With no power on campus, many students went home until classes
could resume. This disruption to campus life was challenging, but
students kept high spirits, displayed resilience, and demonstrated faith
in Christ. Almost immediately, students volunteered to clean up debris,
repair damages, and distribute food on and off campus. Students spoke of
the unexpected blessings that emerged through this experience; they
shared stories of spontaneous prayer meetings and worship sessions
declaring the goodness of God; and they expressed how being offline
encouraged them to fellowship with friends and other believers. The
University reopened the following Wednesday, November 2.

BJU's Community Service Council partnered with ARCH Ministries to
provide relief from Hurricane Helene in the Upstate and North Carolina.
Many BJU students aided in relief efforts in Asheville, North Carolina,
which sustained widespread devastation. The volunteers served in three
ministries: the work at Cornerstone Baptist Church included distributing
supplies and cleaning the facilities; the work at Excel College focused
on unloading trucks of supplies; and the work at Swannanoa Baptist
Church involved organization of supplies and debris cleanup at members'
houses.

In the wake of Hurricane Helene, the Bob Jones University community
rallied together. As students, faculty, staff, and alumni joined in
prayer and support, the storm's damage sparked unity among God's people
and compassion for those in need.
