Artist Series

Coordinated by the School of Fine Arts and Communication, Artist Series
programs bring to campus an array of internationally renowned musical
and dramatic productions. These programs also foster connections with
guest artists who coach students in music, art, and theater.

In September, world-renowned Irish violinist Gregory Hamilton captivated
audiences with his expressive lyricism, beauty of tone, and impeccable
musicianship. Harrington---accompanied by the Emerald
Strings---performed classical pieces, traditional Irish tunes, and works
by twentieth-century composers. What viewer can forget a cello
extravaganza!

Hailing from Windsor Castle, *a cappella* group The Queen's Six are
resident performers at St. George's Chapel. In October the group
presented a delightful night of choral music.

Edmond Rostand's French classic *Cyrano de Bergerac* returned to
Rodeheaver stage in November. The BJU community was delighted by the
swashbuckling tale of romance, bravery, and virtue.

BJU's combined choirs celebrated the Christmas season by performing
Handel's *Messiah*. The biblical accounts of Christ's birth and ministry
came to life through this well-known and celebrated oratorio.

With warmth, wit, and surprising humor, the famed "Nazi Hunter" Simon
Wiesenthal recounted the remarkable story of how, after cheating death
in World War II, he dedicated his life to tracking down war criminals.
This one-man show, written and performed by Tom Dugan, left audiences
breathless.

*The Sound of Music*, one of the most beloved musicals of all time,
delighted audiences in March. Set in Austria, 1938, an exuberant young
governess brings music and restores joy to a broken family, only to face
danger and intrigue as the Nazis gain power.

Living Gallery: *This Same Jesus* combined original drama, music, and
breathtaking live portrayals of classic works of art to celebrate the
story of Jesus. Where do believes hide when their lives are shaken by
thunder? "This Same Jesus" offers shelter during the storms of life.
